Here are some scientific facts on the benefits of strength training for health and longevity:

  1. Muscle Mass Preservation:
    • Strength training helps preserve and increase muscle mass, which is crucial for overall mobility, balance, and functionality, particularly as individuals age.
  2. Bone Density Improvement:
    • Resistance training contributes to enhanced bone density, reducing the risk of osteoporosis and fractures. It strengthens bones by placing stress on them, promoting bone health.
  3. Tomatoes and Cancer Prevention:
    • Tomatoes contain lycopene, an antioxidant associated with a reduced risk of certain cancers, particularly prostate cancer.
  4. Metabolism Boost:
    • Building and maintaining muscle through strength training can boost metabolism. Muscle tissue burns more calories at rest than fat tissue, supporting weight management.
  5. Weight Control:
    • Regular strength training is effective for weight control and fat loss. It promotes a leaner body composition by increasing muscle mass and reducing body fat percentage
  6. Improved Insulin Sensitivity:
    • Resistance training enhances insulin sensitivity, assisting in better blood sugar control. It is beneficial for individuals with or at risk of type 2 diabetes.
  7. Joint Health:
    • Strengthening muscles around joints provides support and stability, reducing the risk of injuries and promoting joint health.
